What Is the Programme Lifecycle?

Lifecycle: Defines the interrelated steps, stages, phases, or processes that provide a structure for governing the progression of work.

The programme lifecycle supports the governance of the programme by guiding orderly progression with clear decision criteria, and ensures that the principles and themes are continually addressed across the programme lifecycle. The seven processes below are what that lifecycle is made of.

Key Roles

The governance boards and individual roles in the Organization theme

Authority flows from the top down; accountability flows from the bottom up. The Sponsoring Group delegates authority to the Programme Board, which the SRO chairs. The SRO is personally accountable to the Sponsoring Group for the programme's success, and delegates day-to-day delivery to the Programme Manager and day-to-day adoption to the Business Change Manager, both of whom are supported by the Programme Office.

How the Key Documents Fit Together

The build order below is the real activity sequence from the "Design the Outcomes" process (not just a logical guess) — the vision is written first, benefits are mapped and then profiled, risks are captured, the target operating model is developed, and the business case is assembled last, from the risk, cost, and benefit information already gathered.
